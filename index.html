Detailed Termite & Pest Control Services That Offer Lasting Results, Guaranteed

At High Point Termite, we provide a holistic approach to pest and termite management, transcending mere symptom treatment to deliver comprehensive, long-term solutions. Comprehensive Termite Control Program

Our termite control program is an exhaustive, multi-stage process engineered to detect, eradicate, and prevent subterranean termites, the most destructive termite species in North Carolina. This program integrates cutting-edge technology with proven methodologies to ensure your property remains free from these silent destroyers.

Initial Comprehensive Termite Inspection

Every effective termite control strategy begins with a thorough inspection. Our certified termite specialists conduct an exhaustive assessment of your property, both interior, and exterior, looking for tell-tale signs of termite activity. This includes:

  • Mud Tubes: Identifying these distinct tunnels constructed by subterranean termites for protection and travel. We check foundations, crawl spaces, basements, and exterior walls.
  • Wood Damage: Tapping and probing wooden structures to detect hollow sounds indicative of internal damage. We inspect joists, beams, subflooring, door frames, and window sills.
  • Frass: Locating termite droppings, which resemble sawdust, particularly for drywood termites (though less common in High Point, we remain vigilant).
  • Discarded Wings: Finding shed wings near windows, doors, or light sources, a common sign of a swarming event.
  • Moisture Sources: Identifying and advising on areas with excessive moisture, which are highly attractive to termites. This includes leaky pipes, poor drainage, and damp crawl spaces.
  • Foundation Cracks & Gaps: Examining any potential entry points in your home’s foundation or exterior.
  • Landscaping Proximity: Assessing if wood-to-ground contact from landscaping, mulch, or woodpiles is creating an inviting environment for termites.

This meticulous inspection allows us to pinpoint the species of termite, the extent of the infestation, and the most vulnerable areas of your home, forming the bedrock of a targeted treatment plan.

Targeted Termite Treatment Applications

Based on our findings, we deploy a tailored treatment strategy designed for maximum efficacy and minimal environmental impact. Our primary methods include:

  • Liquid Termiticide Barriers: We create an imperceptible, non-repellent liquid barrier around your home’s foundation. Termites unknowingly enter this treated zone, pick up the termiticide, and transfer it back to the colony, leading to colony elimination. We utilize industry-leading products like Termidor or Altriset, known for their efficacy and long-lasting residual effects. This application method is precise and safe when applied by our trained professionals.
  • Baiting Systems: For certain situations, or as a supplementary measure, we deploy advanced termite baiting systems. These stations contain a cellulose matrix impregnated with a slow-acting insect growth regulator. Termites feed on the bait, share it with the colony, and gradually, the growth disruptor prevents them from molting, leading to the collapse of the colony. These systems offer continuous monitoring and protection.
  • Direct Wood Treatment: For localized infestations or specific areas of concern, we may apply termiticides directly to infested wood or critical structural components. This provides immediate protection and prevents further damage.

Each treatment is executed with precision, adhering to strict safety guidelines and industry best practices, ensuring powerful results without compromising the safety of your home’s occupants or the environment.

Ongoing Termite Monitoring & Prevention

Our commitment to your home’s protection doesn’t end with treatment. Termite control is an ongoing process. We offer:

  • Annual Inspections: Regular follow-up inspections are crucial to ensure the long-term effectiveness of our treatments and to detect any new activity early.
  • Bait Station Monitoring: For homes utilizing baiting systems, we conduct periodic checks and replenish bait as needed, maintaining continuous protective coverage.
  • Moisture Management Advice: We provide expert recommendations on reducing conditions conducive to termite activity, such as improving drainage, fixing leaks, and enhancing crawl space ventilation.
  • Perimeter Maintenance: Advising on trimming shrubs away from the foundation and removing wood-to-ground contact points that could invite termites.

This proactive approach helps prevent future infestations, saving you from costly repairs down the line and extending the lifespan of your property.

Common Problems & Signs You Need Pest & Termite Control – Lasting Results, Guaranteed

Living in High Point, NC, means sharing our beautiful environment with a variety of fascinating wildlife. However, when nature’s creatures decide to move indoors or directly impact your property, they quickly transform from interesting neighbors into significant problems. Recognizing the early signs of a pest or termite infestation is crucial for timely intervention, preventing minor issues from escalating into major, costly damages or health hazards. High Point Termite helps you understand these warning signs, emphasizing that immediate action is key to lasting results.

Termite Infestation Warning Signs

Termites are often called ‘silent destroyers’ because they can cause extensive damage without any immediate, visible signs. However, diligent observation can reveal early indications:

Clicking Sounds in Walls

This is a subtle but alarming sign. If you press your ear against a wall and hear faint clicking or rustling noises, it could indicate worker termites actively chewing through wood. Termites are quite loud when they’re feasting on your home’s structure. This noise often arises from their mandibles at work, grinding away at the wood fibers. While it might sound like a distant static or a quiet rustle, a consistent or increasing sound warrants immediate investigation. This is particularly noticeable in quiet areas of your home, especially late at night when other household noises are minimal. Prompt action upon hearing these sounds can differentiate between minor localized damage and a widespread structural problem, saving thousands in potential repair costs.

Hollow-Sounding Timber

Termites consume wood from the inside out, often leaving a thin veneer of timber or paint remaining. When you tap on an infested area, it will produce a dull, hollow sound, distinct from the solid thud of healthy wood. This is a clear indicator that the structural integrity of the wood has been compromised. Check door frames, window sills, baseboards, and any exposed wooden beams. This ‘hollow sound’ test is a quick and effective way to identify potential termite damage. The longer an infestation goes unnoticed, the more extensive the hollowing becomes, weakening the building’s framework and increasing the risk of collapse in severe cases. Professional inspection will confirm the extent of the damage without causing further structural weakening.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

As termites continue to consume the internal structure of your home, the affected timber weakens progressively. This weakening can manifest as noticeable sagging in floors, ceilings, or even walls. The floor might feel spongy or uneven underfoot, and ceilings could show signs of bowing or cracking in an unusual pattern. This symptom indicates a significant and widespread termite infestation that has likely been active for an extended period, leading to substantial structural damage. Such conditions compromise the safety of your home and demand urgent professional assessment. Delaying action can lead to catastrophic structural failure, making repairs exponentially more expensive and complex than proactive treatment.

Tight-Fitting Doors or Windows

When termites tunnel through door or window frames, the moisture they introduce, combined with the hollowing out of the wood, can cause the frames to warp and swell. This results in doors and windows becoming difficult to open or close, seemingly ‘sticking’ within their frames. While changes in humidity can also cause wood to expand, if this symptom occurs alongside other potential termite signs, it’s a strong indicator of an infestation. This warping is a direct consequence of the structural changes occurring within the timber, subtly altering the dimensions of the openings. Addressing this issue promptly can prevent further distortion and potential replacement of expensive fixtures.

Visible Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites, the most prevalent and damaging species in High Point, construct distinctive mud tubes to travel safely between their nests (often underground) and their food source (your home). These pencil-sized tunnels are typically found on foundation walls, piers, subflooring, or even emerging directly from the ground. They protect termites from predators and dry environments. The presence of mud tubes is a definitive sign of active termite infestation and requires immediate attention. Even if they appear abandoned, it’s safer to assume activity until a professional confirms otherwise. Damaging these tubes might reveal live termites or indicate where they are actively tunneling, providing valuable clues for targeted treatments.

Discarded Wings

Termite swarmers, or ‘alates,’ are winged reproductive termites that emerge from mature colonies to establish new ones, particularly during warm, humid periods. After successfully mating, they shed their wings. Finding small piles of translucent, discarded wings near windowsills, doorways, or light fixtures is a common and concerning sign of a nearby termite colony. These wings resemble small fish scales and are often mistaken for ant wings, though ant wings differ in shape and size. Identifying these wings indicates that a colony has matured and is reproducing, signifying a significant and imminent threat to your property. Their presence confirms active termite reproduction and infiltration attempts.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

While less common with subterranean termites which use mud tubes to expel waste, drywood termites (which are rarer in High Point but still a possibility) leave behind tiny, pellet-like droppings called ‘frass.’ These droppings are often found in small piles near infested wood and vary in color depending on the wood they’re consuming. They resemble sawdust or coffee grounds. If you notice these granular droppings, especially if accompanied by small kick-out holes in wood, it’s a direct indication of drywood termite activity. Though less common than mud tubes, recognizing frass is critical, as drywood termites do not require soil contact, making their detection slightly different from subterranean species. This suggests an active infestation that warrants precise treatment.

Subterranean Tunnels in Wood

Upon closer inspection of damaged wood, particularly if you manage to break open a piece that sounds hollow, you might discover intricate networks of tunnels running along the grain. These galleries are meticulously carved out by termites as they consume the cellulose within the wood, hollowing it out from the inside. This internal network might be invisible from the outside until the damage becomes severe. Discovering these tunnels confirms a progressive termite attack on your home’s structural elements. The presence of these tunnels underscores the insidious nature of termite damage, often remaining unseen until significant harm has already occurred. This requires immediate professional intervention to prevent further structural compromise.

General Pest Infestation Warning Signs

Pests beyond termites also leave tell-tale signs. Swift recognition helps maintain a clean, healthy, and safe living environment.

Unusual Droppings

Finding small, dark droppings in or around specific areas like kitchens, pantries, or basements is a primary indicator of rodent or cockroach activity. Rodent droppings resemble dark grains of rice (mice) or larger pellets (rats), while cockroach droppings are often smaller, resembling coffee grounds or black pepper. The location, size, and quantity of droppings can help identify the type of pest and the severity of the infestation. Fresh droppings suggest active infestations. These droppings are not only unsightly but also pose significant health risks, carrying bacteria and viruses; immediate sanitation and professional intervention are crucial.

Gnaw Marks

Especially noticeable with rodents, gnaw marks are a clear sign of their presence. You might find small chew marks on food packaging, electrical wires, plastic pipes, furniture, or wooden structures like baseboards and door frames. Rodents constantly gnaw to keep their ever-growing incisors filed down and to access food or nesting materials. Fresh gnaw marks will appear lighter in color, indicating recent activity. Damage to electrical wiring is particularly hazardous, posing a significant fire risk. The location and pattern of gnaw marks can help identify the rodent species and their travel paths within your home, guiding effective pest control strategies.
